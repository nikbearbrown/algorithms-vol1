# Figure brief — Chapter 05: Graphs and Graph Search Algorithms

## Recommended figures

### Figure 1 — Adjacency list vs adjacency matrix
**Path:** `../images/chapter-5-representations.jpg`
**Description:** Same small graph (8 vertices, 12 edges) shown three ways: visual graph; adjacency list; adjacency matrix. Memory annotation under each. Caption notes sparse-vs-dense crossover.
**Use:** referenced in §3 (Representations).

### Figure 2 — BFS vs DFS exploration order
**Path:** `../images/chapter-5-bfs-vs-dfs.jpg`
**Description:** Side-by-side panels showing the same graph with vertices numbered in the order BFS and DFS visit them from the same source. BFS shows level-by-level radiation; DFS shows depth-first descent.
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Traversal).

### Figure 3 — Dijkstra trace on a small graph
**Path:** `../images/chapter-5-dijkstra-trace.jpg`
**Description:** Small weighted graph (~6 vertices). Sequence of three or four panels showing the priority-queue state and finalized vertices at each step. Caption explains why non-negative weights are required (relaxation invariant).
**Use:** referenced in §5 (Shortest paths).

### Figure 4 — A\* search on a road network
**Path:** `../images/chapter-5-astar-vs-dijkstra.jpg`
**Description:** Two side-by-side maps of a small region. Left: Dijkstra's explored vertices radiating from source. Right: A\* with great-circle heuristic, explored vertices forming a narrow cone toward the target. Same source and target on both. Caption: "Same answer, fraction of the work."
**Use:** referenced in §5 and §7 (Worked example).

### Figure 5 — Three MST algorithms on the same graph
**Path:** `../images/chapter-5-mst-algorithms.jpg`
**Description:** Same weighted graph (10 vertices, 18 edges). Three panels showing Kruskal, Prim, and Borůvka building the MST in their respective orders. Final tree highlighted in each panel.
**Use:** referenced in §6 (MSTs).
