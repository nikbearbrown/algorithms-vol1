# Figure brief — Chapter 13: Linear Programming

## Recommended figures

### Figure 1 — LP feasible region and optimum
**Path:** `../images/chapter-13-feasible-region.jpg`
**Description:** 2D LP example. Polyhedral feasible region shown as the intersection of half-planes. Objective gradient drawn as an arrow. Level sets of the objective shown as parallel lines. Optimum vertex highlighted. Caption: "The optimum lies at a vertex; simplex walks vertices, interior-point walks the interior."
**Use:** referenced in §3 (Standard form).

### Figure 2 — Simplex vs interior-point trajectories
**Path:** `../images/chapter-13-simplex-vs-interior-point.jpg`
**Description:** Same 2D LP. Two paths drawn: simplex (along edges, vertex to vertex) and interior-point (curving through the interior to the optimum). Captions: "Simplex: vertex hopping. Interior-point: smooth descent."
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Simplex) and §5 (Interior-point).

### Figure 3 — Primal-dual table
**Path:** `../images/chapter-13-primal-dual-table.jpg`
**Description:** Side-by-side primal LP and its dual LP. Variables, constraints, and objective rendered in matched colors so the correspondence is visible. Caption: "Strong duality: optimal values match. Shadow prices: dual = marginal value of constraints."
**Use:** referenced in §6 (Duality).

### Figure 4 — LP family hierarchy
**Path:** `../images/chapter-13-lp-family.jpg`
**Description:** Hierarchy from LP outward: LP (continuous, polynomial) → QP (quadratic objective, polynomial if convex) → SOCP → SDP → general convex (each containing the previous). On a separate axis: LP → IP → MIP (integer constraints, NP-hard). Each box annotated with example problems.
**Use:** referenced in §7 (LP, IP, MIP, QP — the family).

### Figure 5 — Refinery LP schematic
**Path:** `../images/chapter-13-refinery-lp.jpg`
**Description:** Refinery flowchart. Crude types entering on the left. Distillation paths in the middle (each with capacity). Finished products exiting on the right. Decision variables `x_{ij}` annotated on the path edges. Capacity, demand, and crude-availability constraints labeled. Shadow prices shown as right-margin annotations.
**Use:** referenced in §8 (Worked example).
