# Figure brief — Chapter 06: Greedy Algorithms

## Recommended figures

### Figure 1 — Interval scheduling: three orderings compared
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-interval-scheduling-orderings.jpg`
**Description:** Three timeline strips, same input intervals, three different greedy orderings: earliest start, shortest interval, earliest finish. Selected intervals highlighted; counts annotated. Caption: "Same input, three greedy choices, three different counts. Only earliest-finish is optimal."
**Use:** referenced in §3 (Interval scheduling).

### Figure 2 — Interval partitioning: depth as lower bound
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-partitioning-depth.jpg`
**Description:** Stacked timeline showing meetings as bars, with vertical lines marking the simultaneous-meeting count at peak. Annotation: "depth = 4 → minimum 4 rooms required → greedy achieves 4."
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Interval partitioning) and §7 (Worked example).

### Figure 3 — MST trio on the same graph
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-mst-trio.jpg`
**Description:** Same weighted graph (10 vertices, 18 edges), three panels showing edge-selection sequences for Kruskal (sorted by weight), Prim (growing from one vertex), Borůvka (per-component cheapest in parallel phases). Final MST identical in all three.
**Use:** referenced in §5 (MSTs). Could be shared with Ch 5.

### Figure 4 — Huffman tree construction
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-huffman-tree.jpg`
**Description:** Sequence of priority-queue states. Initial frequencies (a=5, b=9, c=12, d=13, e=16, f=45). Three or four panels showing successive merges. Final tree with codes labeled at leaves.
**Use:** referenced in §6 (Huffman).

### Figure 5 — Coin change counterexample
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-coin-change-counterexample.jpg`
**Description:** Two panels for making 12 cents with denominations (1, 6, 10). Left: greedy = 10 + 1 + 1 (3 coins). Right: optimal = 6 + 6 (2 coins). Caption: "Greedy fails when denominations break the matroid structure."
**Use:** referenced in §8 (Failure modes).
